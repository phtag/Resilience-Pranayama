About Resilience Pranayama

Resilience is the ability to recover from adverse conditions that cause disruption to our sense of balance and well being. This condition can be generated either through practices:


Three natural breaths and one deep breath to begin.

Then,

breathe in — energize

breathe out — let go /do this 5 times


breathe in — feel body sensations

out — let go /5 times


breathe in — everything is possible

breathe out — let go /5 times


breathe in — expand

breathe out — feel body sensations /5 times


Most helpful when energy has been sapped by disappointment, nasty surprises, emails, difficult conversation, being in ‘not knowing’, etc. These kind of events cause the amygdala to excrete cortisol, the stress hormone. This is caused by the reptilian part of the brain alarming the whole system of survival threat. This alarm rushes through the vagus nerve and distributes throughout the entire body.

When lasting over time, this state of stress becomes chronic, and feels just normal. But we are sleep- and energy- sapped and feel down, with subconscious anxiety running like a current through our system.

Not only does this lead to emotional depression, but in this state our brain does not function optimally. Principle sectors affected negatively are the hypo campus — our memory center, the pre-frontal lobe — our executive functions that keep us within the bounds of acceptable behavior.

All this anxiety and stress becomes instantly affected by breathing and mindful practices, generating relaxation and the spaciousness to step back and consider the depth of the moment more granularly. Neurologists have said that the brain seems to operate most optimally in a relaxed state.

Another big shift that happens with practice is that we diffuse our ‘default mode network’, which gives us access to ‘thinking outside the box’ and being relieved of excessive identification constriction.

In brief, with relaxation and focus, accompanied by grounded balance in the body, we we can have the clarity to look deeper into conditions and respond wisely, rather than reactively.
